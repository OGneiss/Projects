Walter Payton

Sweetness

Walter Payton's life:

  • 1954 - Born in Columbia, Mississippi, on July 25th
  • 1971 - Graduates from Columbia High School, where his football career started.
  • 1971-75 - Attended Mississippi's Jackson State University
  • 1972 - Scored 46 points in a single game
  • 1974 - Named Little All-American after setting nine school records at Jackson State College
  • 1975 - Drafted by the Chicago Bears in first round of NFL draft
  • 1976 - Marries Connie Norwood on July 7th
  • 1976-1981 - Won the NFL rushing title for five straight years
  • 1977 - Leads the NFC with 96 points, named NFL Player of the Year and named Sporting News NFC Player of the Year
  • 1984 - Breaks Jim Brown's career rushing record
  • 1985 - Leads the Chicago Bears to win Superbowl XX
  • 1987 - Retires from professional football
  • 1993 - Inducted into Pro Football Hall of Fame
  • 1999 - Develops rare liver disease and later cancer of the bile duct
  • 1999 - Dies at the age of 45 in South Barrington, Illinois, on November 1st
